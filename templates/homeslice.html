HomeSlice

April 2020

Proposal

Our goal is to provide seamless cash to homeowners while democratizing real estate investing. No monthly principal payments, no interest.

Even a homeowner, individuals can fall on hard times and need cash fast. Inversely, investing in real estate is capital intensive and often reserved for more sophisticated investment professionals. What if there's another way to diversify your portfolio? Welcome to HomeSlice.

Tools

Data Flow

Essentially, when a user enters our website, they will be instructed to input their address that they are interested in selling a share of.

Our application communicates with the backend to produce the value of their home, as listed from Zillow, and what we can offer you upon selling.

Backend

In order to communicate between the frontend and backend, we needed to incorporate Flask that allowed for functionality of our data. Our database was generated from the Zillow API, which we plan on expanding to get a larger range of data.

In Python, we used the data recieved from Zillow, cleaned it to fit our needs, and hosted the data on AWS.

We generated our own algorithm to determine how much of a cash offer a homeowner would recieve. For now, the determining factor was a time series analysis based off of 12 years of median sales prices by zipcode.

We incorporated a logistic regression to showcase if the home was a risky investment or not, with a 99% prediction accurancy.

Frontend

Incorporating the Flask aspect, we were able to redirect links to their respective html pages. The functionality of the website was heavily used with Bootstrap to remain responsive across multiple devices.

Typed.js and Isotope.js were prominent JavaScript libraries that we utilized. HTML form submission attributes were required to connect from the frontend to the backend.

Our submission forms were capable of taking in user data and creating a backend database through PostgreSQL. This information allowed us to generate a Tableau dashboard of which property were logged in and to visually see whether this was a risky or solid investment.

Our submission form also takes in user data when they signup in order to create an account profile. In later versions of this venture, we will be creating accounts for buyers and sellers, both featuring dashboards to track investments.
